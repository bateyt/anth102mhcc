Lesson 4 Discussion: Archaeology Today

ANTH 102-W1

As mentioned in the lecture slideshow, archaeology today is no longer confined to the halls of academia. Offices like that of Indiana Jonesa fictional representation of a early/mid-20th-century archaeologistare not part of todays archaeology. In fact, Jones mantra It belongs in a museum is not necessarily always the case, either. Recall that weve mentioned that not all people have the same perceptions of the past. This leads to differences of opinion in how archaeological resources should be treated. Nonetheless, they certainly wouldnt be stored haphazardly on open shelves, like in Indys office!


Instructions

To successfully participate in and complete this discussion activity, you will need to do the following:

  1. Create an initial thread in which you address the question below.
    • In your own words: What benefits and challenges can come to the archaeologist by working with local people and descendant communities? In your answer, you should consider what you have learned about public archaeology, the controversy surrounding Kennewick Man, and the article Digging up Memories. (See Getting Started section below.)
    • Comment on one of the storieslinked in the Lecture Info module pagerelated to protecting archaeological resources (e.g., ISIS destruction, Burns Paiute and the Malheur occupation)
  2. Submit a response post to at least two classmates initial threads. You may address any part of the intial thread related to public archaeology and/or the protection of archaeological and cultural resources. Remember, in framing your response, think about the following:
    • What was your classmates main point? Do you agree or disagree, and why?
    • Did your classmates comments challenge you to think about things that you may have not considered, or reconsider from a different perspective? How so?
    • Is there anything you may contribute to your classmates post?

Evaluation Criteria

You will be evaluated using the same criteria as other discussion activities:

  • Content: There is no required word count, per se, but you must adequately address the questions posed and provide a meaningful, well-thought response. Did you answer the discussion question (and reply) with a thoughtful response that includes references to material presented in the lecture, readings, and/or web-readings?

  • Grammar/style: Did you use proper grammar and style (spelling, complete sentences) making your post easy to understand?

  • Participation: Did you submit the required number of posts. Initial thread and response posts must be made on three separate days. [Students must submit their initial threads by the end-of-day Thursday (at the latest) and your response posts should come over the rest of the week. You may submit more than the minimum required.]

  • Follow the general guidelines of discussion etiquette (see Syllabus).

Getting Started

  1. Before you create that first thread

  • If you have not already, read over lecture readings related to public archaology and the protection of archaeological resources.

  • Do some background reading on the human remains known as Kennewick Man, or The Ancient One.

  • Read up on community archaeology in the article Digging up Memories.

  1. Access the Discussion Forum, Archaeology Today and follow the instructions above.
